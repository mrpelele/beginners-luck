RAINBOW SIX SIEGE

Take control of an Operator in this tactical first person shooter where you and 4 teammates will fight against another team of 5 in a close-quarter objective based break-in. Attack or defend a defined objective, execute the perfect assault or build the ultimate defence, interact with highly destructive maps utilizing specialized gadgets exclusive to each operator and utilize a big repertoire of weapons and tools that accommodate to your preferred playstyle

What's this about

Rainbow six simulates a training session of different police groups around the world. Each one of this individuals posses specialized skills that made them develop certain techniques and gadgets to perform their job the best they can. Choose one of those characters and learn the basics of the game. With time, you will catch the feel of how weapons and items work, we will still give you a basic crash-course to push you in the right direction




There is no standard map in Rainbow six

You will go through lots of different environments

Your objective is to defuse or protect the bomb

For that you will need to go through their defences or stop them from going through yours

ATTACKERS
DEFENDERS

Rainbow six has 2 kinds of playable characters: Attackers and defenders. As the name gives it away, one group specializes in entering the building that is being defended by another group. Although everyone has approximately the same life and speed, the most important aspect that differentiates one team with the other is the specificity of their exclusive gadgets. One side wants to enter a room by scouting information, the other tries to stay invisible, one group tries to breach a wall, the other builds a reinforced one to stop that breaching, and so on and so forth. Choose the one you like with your playstyle in mind. ¿You want to blow up a room? Play fuze, ¿You would like this specific room to be inaccessible to the enemy team? Play mute or castle. There are a lot of different choices to be made

There are 2 in-game phases

Preparation phase, where defenders deploy their various traps, reinforce points of importance and prepare for the incoming attackers that meanwhile can't control any character but are able to deploy drones to search for information about the whereabouts of the enemy team and the location of the objective

Action phase, where the attackers drop into the map and start making their way to the objective using the environment and their gadgets, while defenders wait for them with their advantageous position and the utilization of remote cameras to know at all times the movement of the opponent and their choice of entrance
